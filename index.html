Cognitive Dissonance The #1 tool for mitigating the effects of cognitive dissonance in the workplace

We feel that we are in control, but we are not. The common perception is that we humans use reasoning to make informed decisions. Scientists have discovered, however, that most decisions are made subconsciously, and reasoning enters the picture only when we need to justify our actions. When a problem reaches a certain degree of complexity, our brains simply cannot keep track of it anymore.

When there is a disparity between an action we have committed and the mental models we have available to make sense of them, cognitive dissonance occurs. Cognitive dissonance registers in the anterior cingulate cortex, where the brain also processes sensations of pain. This is why cognitive dissonance is a stressful condition that, when prolonged, can lead to severe anxiety, mental fatigue, and decreased productivity. To reduce the effects of cognitive dissonance in today’s hectic work environment, we present a solution: Justify.ai

With Justify.ai you no longer need to face problematic situations alone. By harnessing cutting edge Large Language Model (LLM) technology we can provide you with an ethical decision-making companion that can be configured to suit your values. Wherever you go, Justify.ai will be there to make sure that you feel like you are on track.

Client CaseImproving the workplace well-being of neurodiverse employees in The Finnish Broadcasting Corporation
Client Case

HR identified that the neuro-diverse staff members of YLE felt excluded from the process of resolving workplace disputes.

Partaking in negotiations with HR felt especially difficult among employees who have difficulty verbalizing their emotions in social situations.

Solution

Justify.ai was configured to provide the neurodivergent staff members a tool for private reflection, without the need for social contact.

Justify.ai provided a stress-free environment for one-to-one counselling and suggested possible courses of action that could then be later applied in face-to-face meetups.
